VTN principles

Practice with restraint and attention.

Sit twice

Use the day as a rhythm: one quiet sit in the morning, one in the late day, returning again and again.

Reduce craving

Notice impulses without immediately obeying them. Let attention settle before choosing the next action.

Return to nature

Use air, shade, stillness, and natural sound as reminders that the mind and environment are not separate.

Source note

Built from public interviews and talks.

This experience is an interpretation of Vo Trong Nghia's publicly described meditation philosophy, especially his emphasis on daily sitting, reconnecting with nature, and practicing with ethical discipline. It is not presented as an official course or endorsed program.
